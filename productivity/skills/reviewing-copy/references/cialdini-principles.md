# Cialdini's 7 Principles of Persuasion

Use these as a checklist when reviewing copy. Most pages use 1–2 well; great pages stack 3–4 without feeling forced.

## 1. Reciprocity

People feel obliged to return value they've received.

- Lead with a free guide, calculator, template, or insight before the ask.
- The gift must feel personal and useful, not a thinly disguised lead magnet.
- Look for: "free", "no signup", "download", value delivered before any form.

## 2. Commitment & Consistency

Small "yeses" make bigger ones easier.

- Micro-commitments: quizzes, configurators, "show me my plan" buttons.
- Public statements (reviews, shared progress) lock in behavior.
- Look for: low-friction first steps, personalization that feels chosen, not assigned.

## 3. Social Proof

People copy what similar others do.

- Specific testimonials > generic praise. Names, photos, roles, outcomes.
- "Join 12,847 founders" beats "join thousands".
- Logos of recognizable customers. Counts. Reviews. Case studies.
- Beware of vague proof ("loved by teams everywhere") — it reads as having none.

## 4. Authority

Credentials, expertise, and signals of competence shift trust.

- Founder credentials, press mentions, certifications, in-product data.
- Show, don't tell — "as featured in" with logos beats "we're trusted".
- Specific numbers ("processed $2.4B in payments") beat adjectives.

## 5. Liking

People buy from people (and brands) they like.

- Conversational tone, human warmth, founder voice.
- Mirror the audience's language and worldview.
- Photos of real humans, not stock. Behind-the-scenes. Honest stories.

## 6. Scarcity

Limited availability raises perceived value — but only when legitimate.

- Real deadlines, real seat counts, real cohort closes.
- Fake scarcity (countdown timers that reset) destroys trust on detection.
- "Only 12 spots left this month" only works if it's actually true.

## 7. Unity

Shared identity is stronger than mere liking — it's "we" instead of "I like you".

- Tribe signaling: "for indie hackers", "built by engineers, for engineers".
- Shared enemies, shared values, shared vocabulary.
- The strongest form of social proof: "people like me use this".

## Stacking guidance

- Hero section: usually Authority + Social Proof + Unity.
- Pricing section: Scarcity + Commitment + Reciprocity (free trial).
- Testimonials: Social Proof + Liking + Authority (if customer is credentialed).
- CTA buttons: Commitment + low-friction yeses.
