# Copywriting Frameworks

Pick the framework that fits the format and reader state. Don't force a long-form structure onto a 50-word ad.

## AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action

Best for: long-form sales pages, video sales letters, multi-section landing pages.

- **Attention** — Stop the scroll with a headline that promises a dramatic benefit, evokes curiosity, or names a specific pain.
- **Interest** — Build the reader's stake in continuing. Stories, surprising facts, "you're not alone" framing.
- **Desire** — Show the transformation. Specific outcomes, proof, social validation, vivid future-pacing.
- **Action** — One clear next step, restated benefit, risk reversal, urgency.

When AIDA fails: short copy. There isn't room for all four beats — you'll dilute each.

## PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solution

Best for: ads, cold outreach, short copy, paid social, email subject + opener.

- **Problem** — Name the pain in the reader's own words. Specific, not generic.
- **Agitate** — Twist the knife. What does this pain cost them? What happens if it continues?
- **Solution** — The relief, framed as the obvious next step.

When PAS fails: cold audiences who don't yet know they have the problem. Use BAB or curiosity-led hooks instead.

## BAB — Before, After, Bridge

Best for: storytelling-driven copy, case studies, testimonials, transformation pitches.

- **Before** — The reader's current state (or a relatable customer's). Make it vivid.
- **After** — The desired state. Specific, concrete, emotionally loaded.
- **Bridge** — Your offer is the bridge between the two.

When BAB fails: when there's no clear "before" pain — e.g., utility products, B2B tools where the status quo isn't painful enough to dramatize.

## Picking between them

| Format | Default |
|---|---|
| Tweet, headline, ad, subject line | PAS |
| Hero section | PAS or BAB |
| Landing page | AIDA or BAB |
| Long sales page | AIDA |
| Case study | BAB |
| Cold email | PAS |
